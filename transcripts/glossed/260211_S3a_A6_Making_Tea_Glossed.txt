# Interlinear Glossed Text

Language:
Wakhi

Text:
A6 - Making Tea

Method:

Morpheme-by-morpheme glossing following Leipzig Glossing Rules.


Sentence 1

Lexical line:

ska ragnig-eʃ zerdig-ʃ ska din ha yuk-ʃ ra kadin


Gloss:

pot fire-? water-? pot LOC put


Free translation:

You put the pot on the fire and pour water in.


---

Sentence 2

Lexical line:

aval dangk-ti tʃai ra kadin


Gloss:

first tea-? tea LOC put


Free translation:

First, put the tea in the pot.


---

Sentence 3

Lexical line:

yan-iʃ woz zak liter-in mat vita zak ha peʃt


Gloss:

then little while hot become boil


Free translation:

Then it is left for a little while and it boils.


---

Sentence 4

Lexical line:

pisak ʃiru vitja


Gloss:

cook start become


Free translation:

Then the cooking starts.


---

Sentence 5

Lexical line:

yadge-ʃ ra kadin zak woz padze


Gloss:

milk-? LOC put little more cook


Free translation:

There you pour in a little more milk and let it cook.


---

Sentence 6

Lexical line:

yan-iʃ tʃai teor wost


Gloss:

then tea ready become


Free translation:

Then the tea is ready.


# Analysis Notes

Important forms analysed during the project:

-eʃ, -ʃ, -iʃ:

Interpreted cautiously as filler/discourse-like suffixes where grammatical function could not be established.

ra:

Analysed as locative/postposition.

vita, vitja, wost:

Analysed as light verbs expressing:

"happen"

"come to be"

peʃt:

Used for boiling liquid.

padze and pisak:

Associated with cooking processes.

The consultant's explanations were used to avoid assigning unsupported meanings.
